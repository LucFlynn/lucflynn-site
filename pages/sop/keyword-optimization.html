Google Ads SOP — Keyword Optimization

Field‑tested steps to fix wasted spend, improve relevance, and rebuild winners. Updated Nov 4, 2025

Objective

Align keywords → ads → landing pages, pause under‑performers, and give viable terms a structured “second chance” under tighter controls.

Steps

  1. Pull performance data (14–30 days)
    • Metrics: Impr, Clicks, CTR, Conv, Cost, CPA; segment by Keyword, MatchType, AdGroup.
    • Exclude the last 24–48h if conversion lag is material.
  2. Define under‑performance thresholds

    Examples

    • CPA > target by 25%+ with ≥ 25 clicks
    • CTR < ad group avg by 30%+
    • 0 conversions after 40–60 clicks

    Labels

    • Pause — clear loser
    • 2nd‑Chance — viable w/ tighter controls
    • Scale — clear winner
  3. Fix relevance
    • Ensure keyword appears in RSA headlines/descriptions where natural.
    • Landing page continues the promise (headline, offer, CTA).
    • Move mismatched terms to better ad groups or new pages.
  4. Restructure ad groups
    • Split strong vs weak terms into separate ad groups.
    • Allocate budget to the strong group; throttle the weak/test group.
  5. Pause or 2nd‑chance
    • Pause losers that met thresholds and failed adjustments.
    • 2nd‑chance protocol: tighten match (Broad → Phrase/Exact), adjust bids, refresh ad copy, improve page; review in 7–14 days.
  6. Add negatives
    • Mine Search Terms for irrelevant/wasteful queries (clicks w/ no conv, high cost).
    • Maintain shared negative lists for brand safety and junk modifiers.
  7. Adjust match types & bids
    • Tighten under‑performing Broad into Phrase/Exact; expand winners cautiously.
    • Increase bids on winners within CPA/ROAS guardrails.

Deliverables

  • Keyword status CSV (Active / Paused / 2nd‑Chance / Scale).
  • Ad group restructuring notes (what moved, why).
  • Negative keyword diff (added this round).
  • Bid/budget change log.
  • Before/after snapshot for client.

Suggested Cadence

Weekly quick scan; monthly deep dive. Re‑label 2nd‑Chance terms after each review window.

FAQ

How many clicks before I judge a keyword? ▾

Rule‑of‑thumb: 40–60 clicks for low‑ticket leads; more if CVR is low or laggy. Use judgement and your vertical’s norms.

Should I ever keep Broad Match? ▾

Yes, in well‑controlled environments with strong negatives and proven CVR. Otherwise, tighten until profitable.
