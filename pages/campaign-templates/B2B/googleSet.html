Google Ads Campaign Settings Guide

Essential settings for Search campaigns to maximize performance and control costs

I'll walk you through each of these settings step-by-step

Networks ✓ Recommended

Select: Google Search Network only
Uncheck: Display Network, Search Partners

Why This Matters
Search Network gives you the most control and highest intent traffic. Display and Search Partners often have lower quality clicks and make optimization harder.
Location Targeting ✓ Critical

Select: "Presence: People in or regularly in your targeted locations"
Avoid: "Presence or interest" setting

Why This Matters
The "interest" option shows ads to people just searching for your location, even if they're nowhere near it. This wastes budget on unqualified clicks.
Location Exclusions ✓ Recommended

Add: Areas you don't serve or that underperform
Review: Monthly performance by location

Why This Matters
Block specific cities, zip codes, or regions where you can't deliver service or that consistently waste budget. This prevents irrelevant clicks.
Maximize Clicks ◐ Depends on Business

Local Businesses: Start with this if you're just launching
National/Broader: Avoid - use Manual CPC or Target CPA instead

Why This Matters
For local businesses with tight geo-targeting, Maximize Clicks can work initially to gather data. For national campaigns with broader targeting, it often drives low-quality clicks and wastes budget. Switch to Target CPA once you have conversion data.
Maximize Conversions ◐ Use With Caution

Only Use If: You have 30+ conversions/month consistently
Otherwise: Start with Manual CPC or Target CPA

Why This Matters
Without enough conversion data, this strategy can blow through your budget quickly. Google's algorithm needs sufficient data to optimize properly.
Phrase Match Keywords ✓ Recommended

Use: Phrase match "keyword" as your primary match type
Format: Wrap keywords in quotes: "plumber near me"

Why This Matters
Phrase match gives you balance between reach and control. It matches searches that include your keyword phrase in the right order, filtering out completely irrelevant queries.
Broad Match ✗ Avoid (Initially)

Start Without: Broad match keywords
Later: Test carefully with strong negative keyword lists

Why This Matters
Broad match gives Google too much freedom to interpret your keywords. You'll often show for irrelevant searches. Only use once you have robust conversion tracking and negative keywords.
Negative Keywords ✓ Critical

Add From Day 1: 50-100 negative keywords minimum
Review: Weekly search terms report to add more

Why This Matters
Negative keywords prevent your ads from showing for irrelevant searches (e.g., "free," "DIY," "jobs"). This is your #1 tool to cut wasted spend.
Tracking Template ✓ Advanced

Add: UTM parameters to track campaign performance
Example: ?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign={campaign}

Why This Matters
Tracking templates let you pass campaign data to your analytics platform. This helps you see exactly which campaigns, ad groups, and keywords drive conversions.

Pro Tip: Review and adjust these settings monthly based on performance data
